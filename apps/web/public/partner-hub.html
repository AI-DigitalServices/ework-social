05 — Example Flows & Guidance

These are examples, not scripts

You know your audience better than we do — adapt freely, or ignore entirely and do this your own way.

Sharing with a LinkedIn audience

Some partners mention it naturally in a post about their own workflow ("here's what I use to manage client social media"), rather than a direct pitch. This tends to feel more authentic than an obvious promotional post.

Sharing in a community or WhatsApp group

A short, honest mention — what it is, why you're using it, and the link — works better than a long sales message. Communities respond to genuine recommendations, not pitches.

Tag us when you share

If you post publicly, tagging or mentioning us helps in two directions — it lets us see and support your post (reshares, replies, engagement), and it builds visible credibility for you as one of our Founding Growth Partners.

LinkedIn — @eWork Social Instagram / Facebook — @eworksocial X / Twitter — @eworksocial

Not required — some partners prefer sharing quietly within their own community without a public tag, and that's completely fine too.

A note on privacy and legitimacy: When demonstrating the platform, please don't share real client data, screenshots containing confidential client information, or specific client names without their permission. If you're showing the product live, use your own test content or generic examples. Also — be transparent that this is a partnership. If someone asks whether you earn anything from the referral, say so honestly. Trust with your audience matters more than any single signup.
